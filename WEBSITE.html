Raas Caseyr (Cape Guardafui)

Geography, history, archaeology and the modern province — quick guide for visitors and students.

Overview

Raas Caseyr — also widely known as Cape Guardafui — is the rocky promontory at the tip of the Horn of Africa where the Guardafui Channel meets the Indian Ocean. It marks an important maritime junction between the Gulf of Aden and the wider Indian Ocean.

Antiquity & Trade

(What classical sailors recorded)

Classical sources (e.g., the Periplus of the Erythraean Sea) mention the Horn’s capes and the incense trade routes that linked Somalia’s coast to Arabia and the Roman world. Archaeological finds at nearby Ras Hafun show active maritime trade across the Indian Ocean during the Hellenistic and Roman periods.

19th–20th centuries & the Lighthouse

Local sultanates such as the Majeerteen controlled parts of the northeast coast. During the Italian colonial period a concrete lighthouse — constructed in the early 20th century — was built to reduce shipwrecks near the cape. Remnants of colonial-era buildings still exist.

Modern Administration & Economy

Raas Caseyr sits inside Puntland, an autonomous region of Somalia. The local economy depends on fishing and traditional exports such as frankincense and myrrh. In recent decades Puntland has reorganised provinces and districts in the Bari area; local governance and infrastructure continue to evolve.

Timeline (quick)

1st century CE — Mention in the Periplus and active trade in the region.
Medieval era — Somali coastal trade with Arabia and the Red Sea.
Early 20th century — Colonial-era lighthouse constructed.
2013–2023 — Administrative changes in Puntland affecting the surrounding districts and province names.
